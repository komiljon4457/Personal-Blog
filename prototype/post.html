Three days in the Dolomites without a car

12 July 2026 · by Anna Weber · in Travel · 6 min read

Public transport in the Italian Alps is better than its reputation. Over a long weekend I linked together three mountain huts using nothing but regional buses, a cable car, and my own two feet — and never once wished for a car.

Day one: getting to the trailhead

From Dobbiaco station, the 445 bus runs hourly up to the Auronzo hut. I bought the ticket on board, watched the valley fall away below, and was walking on the ridge by early afternoon.

Day two: the high traverse

This was the long day — nine hours across scree and past the old wartime tunnels. The huts along the way meant I could travel light and still eat well every evening.

What I’d do differently

Book the huts earlier. In July the popular ones fill up weeks ahead, and a car would not have helped with that at all.
